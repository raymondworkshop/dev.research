Effective social interaction involves distinguishing between necessary social duties and the optional selection of friends. While we must cooperate with everyone for the common good, we should be selective about our inner circle to avoid being corrupted by toxic habits.

## Strategies for Difficult People
- **Selective Friendship**: Avoid friends with destructive habits or values.
- **Social Fatalism**: Accept that some people will naturally be annoying or dislike us; treat their behavior as an inevitable part of nature.
- **Perspective**: View annoying events against the vast background of the universe to diminish their perceived importance.
- **Avoid Hatred**: The greatest risk in dealing with difficult people is becoming hateful ourselves, which causes us internal harm.

## Differentiating Relationships
Distinguish between "transactional" relationships (useful but limited) and deep, long-term partnerships to protect personal energy.
