Stoics believe that humans are inherently social animals with a duty to cooperate with others for the common good. This responsibility persists even when dealing with difficult or annoying people, as functioning as part of the human "organism" is our natural purpose.

## Key Principles
- **Mutual Benefit**: Humans are designed to live in society and interact in ways that help others.
- **The Human Function**: Doing what we are "built to do" (fulfilling our social role) leads to true joy.
- **Tolerance**: We must work with difficult people because they are part of the same human community.
