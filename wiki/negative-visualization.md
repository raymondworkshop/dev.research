Negative visualization, or "premeditatio malorum," is the practice of imagining the loss of things we value. This Stoic exercise helps us appreciate what we currently have while preparing us for the possibility of its disappearance.

### 消極想像 (Negative Visualization)
*   **預想壞事發生**: 定期花時間想像失去自己所珍視的事物。
*   **阻斷不滿足**: 搶先阻斷我們永遠都不能滿足於自己的生活的心態。
*   **享受而不依附**: 教導我們享受已擁有的東西，又不依附（Nonattached）它們。
    *   擁抱自己恰巧擁有的人生，並從中汲取每一分欣喜。
    *   對於奪走令我們快樂的事物的變化，要有心理準備。

### Emotional Freedom
Being nonattached creates emotional freedom. This suggests trying hard and really caring, but being completely willing to let go of the outcome. 
*   "I'll do everything possible... But, If I don't, that's okay, too."

### Living Each Day
*   **把每一天都當成最後一天來過**: 欣賞這一天活著的精彩，用行動來填滿這一天，改變行動時的心智狀態。
