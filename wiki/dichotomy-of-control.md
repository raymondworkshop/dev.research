The dichotomy of control is a fundamental Stoic practice of categorizing life events into things we can and cannot influence. By focusing energy only on what is within our power, we avoid unnecessary frustration and disappointment.

## Three Categories of Control
1. **Complete Control**: Our own goals and values.
2. **Partial Control**: Situations where we can influence the outcome but not guarantee it (e.g., winning a match).
3. **No Control**: External events or the past, which should be set aside without worry.

## Goal Internalization
Stoics suggest turning external goals into internal ones. Instead of "winning a game" (external), the goal becomes "playing to the best of my ability" (internal), which is entirely within one's control.
